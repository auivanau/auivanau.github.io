# The Lab — Outreach Templates

This file is intentionally not published by Jekyll. Personalize every message. Do not send these as a bulk campaign.

## Outreach targets

Prioritize people for whom a specific issue is genuinely relevant:

1. Canadian radiation oncology residents and chief residents;
2. radiation oncology program education leads;
3. medical physicists working in adaptive RT, imaging, QA, or AI;
4. clinician-researchers whose work was reviewed;
5. trainee sections of professional societies;
6. departmental or residency newsletters;
7. oncology, medical physics, and clinical-AI newsletter editors;
8. faculty or peers who have previously expressed interest in literature surveillance.

Start with five personalized messages per month.

No need to share it, but I would appreciate any correction if I have misrepresented the methods or conclusions.

Best,
Ivan Au, MD, P.Eng

## Follow-up rule

Do not send repeated follow-ups to people who did not respond. One brief follow-up is reasonable only when:

- the person requested the material;
- there is a new issue directly connected to their work;
- a deadline or newsletter cycle was explicitly discussed.

## Personalization checklist

Before sending, make sure the message contains:

- the recipient’s correct role and name;
- one reason this specific article is relevant to them;
- one direct link rather than the generic homepage;
- no request for endorsement;
- no exaggerated claims about audience size or impact;
- a low-pressure invitation to correct, critique, or use the resource.
