# The Lab — Distribution Playbook

This file is intentionally prefixed with an underscore so Jekyll will not publish it as a public page.

## Objective

Build a small, high-trust professional audience for Radiation Oncology Weekly and Clinical Horizon Scan without creating a second full-time content workload.

The core principle is: **publish once, distribute the useful ideas several times.**

## Weekly publishing rhythm

### Sunday — Full issue

- Publish the complete issue on the site.
- Send the email edition once a newsletter provider is connected.
- Update the canonical article only on the website; all other platforms should link back to it.
- Select three reusable ideas while editing:
  1. strongest clinical signal;
  2. strongest physics/AI/workflow signal;
  3. most important limitation, negative result, or overhyped narrative.

### Monday — The week in 30 seconds

Post a concise summary on LinkedIn:

- one sentence describing the overall importance of the week;
- three specific signals;
- one sentence stating what did **not** change;
- link to the full issue.

Template:

> **Radiation oncology this week:**
>
> • [strongest clinical signal]
> • [workflow/physics signal]
> • [important negative or cautionary finding]
>
> My bottom line: [one calibrated interpretation].
>
> Full ranked review: [link]

### Wednesday — One-paper appraisal

Choose the most discussable paper and publish a self-contained appraisal:

- the clinical or technical question;
- one genuine strength;
- one decisive limitation;
- current readiness: ready now / possibly soon / hypothesis-generating;
- link to the original paper and the full digest.

Tag no more than one or two authors when the appraisal accurately represents their work.

### Friday — Evergreen lesson

Extract one reusable principle that is not dependent on clicking the article.

Examples:

- why external validation is not the same as clinical utility;
- how to distinguish a surrogate endpoint from patient benefit;
- why a negative randomized trial may be more important than a high-performing retrospective model;
- what clinicians should ask before adopting automated contouring.

This is the highest-value format for building recognition over time.

## Monthly rhythm

Once each month:

- publish or advance one evergreen article;
- identify five people or organizations for personalized outreach;
- review analytics and note which topics generated subscriptions, replies, or returning readers;
- update one older gateway article with links to relevant new issues;
- choose one strong issue as the current representative sample for outreach.

## Channel priority

1. **Website:** canonical archive and search destination.
2. **Email:** recurring relationship and highest-value conversion.
3. **LinkedIn:** primary professional discovery channel.
4. **Bluesky or another academic network:** optional secondary channel.
5. **Reddit or professional forums:** participate only when community rules permit and the post is useful without the link.

Do not attempt to maintain every platform.

## Content reuse table

| Source material | Distribution output |
|---|---|
| Bottom Line section | Monday summary post |
| Highest-ranked paper | Wednesday paper appraisal |
| Key limitations | Friday evergreen lesson |
| Evidence table or ranking | Visual carousel or concise text summary |
| Repeated theme over 4–6 issues | Evergreen gateway article |
| Author response or correction | Follow-up discussion post |

## LinkedIn profile positioning

Suggested headline:

> Radiation Oncology Resident | MD, P.Eng | Writing Radiation Oncology Weekly and Clinical Horizon Scan

Suggested featured items:

1. latest Radiation Oncology Weekly issue;
2. methodology page;
3. strongest evergreen article.

## Metrics that matter

Track monthly rather than daily:

- email subscribers;
- returning site visitors;
- newsletter open and click rates;
- posts that lead to subscriptions;
- replies or discussion from clinicians and researchers;
- authors who engage with an appraisal;
- external links, mentions, or inclusion in resource lists.

Follower count and raw impressions are secondary.

## Editorial guardrails

- Do not tag authors primarily to obtain reach.
- Do not imply endorsement by the University of Alberta, Alberta Health Services, or the Cross Cancer Institute.
- Do not reproduce abstracts or copyrighted figures without permission.
- Clearly distinguish preprints, regulatory announcements, and peer-reviewed evidence.
- Correct errors visibly when they could alter interpretation.
- Do not publish identifiable clinical cases.
- Keep promotional posts useful even when the reader never clicks.

## Forty-five-minute weekly distribution checklist

- 10 min: extract three insights while finalizing the issue.
- 10 min: write and schedule the Monday post.
- 10 min: draft the Wednesday appraisal.
- 10 min: draft the Friday evergreen principle.
- 5 min: respond thoughtfully to relevant discussion from the prior week.
